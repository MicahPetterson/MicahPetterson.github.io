Website Evaluations

James Bond 007 Museum

This website serves to promote and advertise for the James Bond 007 Museum, located in Sweden. The site gives the user an expansive view of all that their exhibitions have to offer, and what anyone visiting the museum can expect to see. The website also seems to serve as a general news outlet for the James Bond movies, cast, and anything else related to the franchise.

CRAP

  • Contrast - This site does a rather acceptable job at keeping content in contrast with its context. The background is a solid white, and all of the text is either black or a strong blue, which keeps everything easily discernable for the user.
  • Repetition - The site doesn't do a particularly great job at keeping a consistent pattern with its content. While the site follows a (very, very rough) pattern of images with headings above them, the text color, alignment, and font vary with each occurence.
  • Alignment - Frankly, this website does a pretty terrible job at alignment. Virtually every image has a radically different aspect ratio from the others, the text varies sporadically from center-aligned to left-aligned and everything in between, and any bit of content inside a bordered element is positioned very awkwardly within it.
  • Proximity - Finally, proximity is executed rather atrociously in this website as well. There are no indicators of what kind of groups of content the site could or even should be divided up into. The main page is simply one very long patchwork of various James Bond-related bits of information, with no real distinguishment between each item or how any of them connect to one another in any way.

SCP Foundation Wiki

This is a collaborative sci-fi writing website used to hold the collective works of all participants of the project of the "SCP Foundation". The site stores fiction articles, short stories, and works of art as well as forums dedicated to the purpose of cultivating the community of writers and artists that contribute to the site.

CRAP

  • Contrast - This website does a great job with contrast, keeping a rather consistent theme of either black or dark red on white that clearly distinguishes the content from its background or context. Black or dark red text on a white surface is very easily readable for virtually all users.
  • Repetition - Not only is the color scheme consistent for this site, but so are the more graphical elements. All across the site, the designers utilized repeated visual design to provide a consistent theme that the user can become familiar with as the navigate from page to page.
  • Alignment - The alignment for this site is also executed very well. Each module of information is store in a box that forms a neat gridwork with all of the others, allowing the user's eyes to navigate more smoothly across the page.
  • Proximity - This site also uses proximity very effectively, making sure to group together like pieces of content. For example, the navigation bar, social media links, and actual content are all clearly divided into their own sections on the main page.
